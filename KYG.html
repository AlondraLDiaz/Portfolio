Kill Your Grass

Kill Your Grass is a website that details the benefits of artificial turfgrass and its impact as an alternative to current financial, time, and energy spent into lawn care. Kill your Grass was created to help people make informed choices of how artificial grass can fit into their lifestyle.

Project Overview

Problem

We believe that there is a common denominator when lawn care is discussed. Many want to save time working in the yard so that they can do the things they enjoy. Often money has a huge impact when it comes to maintenance. Many don’t know the alternative lawn options.

Solution

Kill Your Grass provides people holistic information on artificial grass in order to make an informed decision about replacing grass/sod with artificial grass. This knowledge details the benefits of artificial grass on the environment, on financial expenditures and time preservation while being accessible to everyone.

Goal

Research

Objectives

Our research began when we probed ourselves for why artificial grass could be significant and how our users could find this information. We sought to know what motivated people to install artificial grass but what information would our users need to know to even seek out artificial grass. This helped us to narrow our research objectives to the following:

      Identify that we need to discover our users' knowledge of artificial grass.
      Identify users' challenges or barriers to have artificial grass as their lawn care.
      How they felt about the transition and the likelihood of changing their lawn based on their own experiences with lawn care and yard maintenance (emotions & pain points).

Thus, we started our research on our competitor’s to discover what knowledge is already available and how we can develop our site.

Competitor Analysis

We started reviewing educational sites and product sites then evaluating their presentation of information. We conducted a IA heuristic of these sites due to the ample amount of information there is on artificial grass and complexity of presentation along with other features. Our analysis provided us with how we can present and provide our users ease in accessing information and implementing an informed choice laid out on one website.

Proto Persona

After our insight with our research, we needed to preview who Kill Your Grass users would be. We began with sending a survey questionnaire with demographics and general questions about artificial grass and lawn care. The survey results provided us insight on what we needed to start composing our proto persona. This enabled us to understand our users' needs based on their gains and pain points but most importantly why and how our users would seek our site.

User Interviews

With a user in mind, we started our research interview plan with questions developing on each objective. The preliminary survey allowed us to see how our questions can differ and gain a deeper insight to the psychology of our users using open-ended questions while giving us a criteria for who our ideal interviewees are. We recruited five participants to interview to gain an in-depth understanding of our users and confirm our initial comprehension of them from our survey and proto persona. We discover overarching themes which are the following:

      Most users have some knowledge of Artificial grass
      Most users care for yard about 1x a week ( watering varied)
      3:5 said they would get artificial grass
      Time is a common issue
      Cost is the only hesitation about artificial grass

Define & Ideate

We synthesized the data from our survey and interviews to help us start identifying how we can resolve the challenges facing our users goal's when it comes to alleviating lawn care expenditures, time and energy.

Affinity Diagram

We gathered and organized by our insights from our interviews and used an Affinity Diagram to organize our insights. These included their demographics and snippets of comments from our interviews. We scoped for common patterns between our users and conducted further dissection of notes and sorted them into groups.

Empathy Map

After grouping our insights we wanted to gain a deeper understanding of how our user’s empathize with their yard. We used an empathy map to distinguish our user’s modes of perception and how they could connect to artificial grass.

From our deduced data, we discovered the needs for our site to address both pains and gains expressed from our user. However, we desired to stay centered on our specific user so we began with composing a user persona.


User Persona

With the insight from our empathy map and affinity diagram, We wanted to ensure we had a focal user as we began to ideate and know who to design for. We created our user persona Ray Jensen.

Now with a better understanding with who our Kill Your Grass user is, their pains and gains. We gathered our top features and began sketching our wireframes.

Usability Testing

Our usability testing was done remote via Zoom with a moderated Think Aloud method. We recruited five participants to test our design’s functionality by stating their thoughts on how they felt, what they saw, and their actions while going testing the site by completing tasks given. We formulated tasks based on our main pages which were our homepage, resources, gallery and education page. The following feedback we received was to:

      Reduce text and add more white space
      Add a transition or animation to indicate more content on homepage
      Change layout of education pages
      Time is a common issue
      Eliminate sticky navigation

Revisions

We reiterated on our site twice due to functionality on the development front, along with ensuring fluidity of content to the user flow. We added and adjusted the following:

      Adjust FAQs from cards
      Reformatt Resource Cards
      Create Nav with dropdown

Branding

Before applying UI design to our wireframes, we wanted to ensure that the site had a motif. We had begun with the inspiration of calling our site Kill Your Grass but contemplating the tone that could accompany our brand name.

Mood Board

We began seeking inspiration via Google search images and Pinterest. We searched for cutting grass images and mowing the lawn. It is when we stumbled upon our mascot, Rock N’ Roll guy mowing the lawn. This provided us the tone we wanted Kill Your Grass to carry: Edgy, Fresh, Rocking, Eclectic.

Logo Design

Now we wanted the Logo to convey the same tone as our brand and content. We knew we wanted to use a dense font typeface and convey the edge of a cut. We had incorporated these ideas into our elements in the first and second design. However, based on peer feedback we needed to change the way we incorporated our methods to be cleaner and subtle. We decided to change the color to bring the subtle grass in the background and maintain our idea. Along with finding a font that had a dense and edgy element of lines within the letters.

UI Style Guide

After designing our logo and solidifying elements of the Kill your grass brand. We created our style guide with the intention to complement our key UI elements. Then we applied our style guide to our wireframes.

We landed on taking this idea and spinning it to promote artificial grass. We learned a lot about artificial grass and gained better insight from those with lawns on how to approach promoting artificial grass with balancing a product and providing education.

As my team built upon our knowledge of development, we learned that translating design principles to developer practical application is inherently more complex. It requires more than just communicating but to be able to understand the capabilities of software and have a vast knowledge of how to speak in programming languages. As we expand on our future opportunities to develop, we will need to focus on development, content writing and practical technical implementation for the feasibility to have these features added to Kill Your Grass.
