Utah Open Lands

Utah Open Lands is a non-profit organization that protects Utah land in its natural state and prevents any alterations or destruction or construction on the land. Redesigning Utah Open Lands is meant to help captivate and capture new audiences to the cause to engage, participate or donate to maintain the mission of keeping Utah land open to everyone.

Project Overview

Problem

Utah Open Lands users want to easily find information about the nonprofit and donation opportunities. They also feel it is important to know how donations are being used in the organization.

Solution

We believe that adding more content regarding donations, videos and other images from social events and volunteer projects throughout the website will engage current users. Along with updating the color guide, navigation UI/IA + functionality, and creating consistent buttons displayed to captivate new audiences. By adding these elements we can enable people to discover Utah Open Lands by bringing a more modern and informative experience to the UOL website overall.

Research

Objectives

We began our research with investigating what is and isn’t motivating users to continue through the Utah Open Lands (UOL) website to volunteer or donate. We sought to discover the components to what captivates a user to seek UOL and either volunteer/donate. This probing helped us narrow our research objectives to the following:

      Identify design elements that psychological impact user decisions
      Discover how the content helps the user become informed, provide information,
      and feel engaged
      Discover how navigation content and infrastructure provide user with tools
      to search for user needs

Thus, we wanted to get an understanding of who UOL and users are, so we contancted the stakeholders to conduct an interview to gain insights for our research.

Stakeholder Interview

We conducted stakeholder interviews remotely via Zoom in which we had prepared several open-ended questions to be able to capture an in-depth understanding of who UOL was, their users and how they connect. We described our goal to the stakeholders about redesigning their website for the purpose of garnishing more audiences and ease any current user experience with responsiveness and UI updates. Our interview provided details about UOL users by the logistics and financial sectors of their organization.

They are very informed about their users being conveyed in two distinct groups which are landowners and a demographic of 45 years old +. They also distinguished their volunteers from donors being that volunteer demographics are 40 years old and younger. To be portrayed as realistic as possible, the stakeholders recommended we keep the logo and color style consistently the same as their current guide along with documents kept on site, otherwise we could update the rest of the site.

With the insights from our interview, we were ready to conduct research on competitors.

Competitor Analysis

We started reviewing educational sites and product sites then evaluating their presentation of information. We conducted a IA heuristic of these sites due to the ample amount of information there is on artificial grass and complexity of presentation along with other features. Our analysis provided us with how we can present and provide our users ease in accessing information and implementing an informed choice laid out on one website.

Proto Persona

Now that we had an understanding of UOL, we wanted to focus on centering around a user. Therefore we used a survey questionnaire to inform us on how we create our proto persona. This provided us insight as to why and how our users seek out UOL along with their gains and pain points.

User Interviews

With a user in mind, we started our research interview plan with questions developing on each objective. We recruited five participants to interview to gain an in-depth understanding of our users and confirm our initial comprehension of them from our survey and proto persona. We discovered gains and pain points from our interviews and quoted in the following:

      “The give tab is very overwhelming, too many options and not enough description about what it is.”

      “It seems like they put a lot of effort into trying to get donations without spending the time to convince me what my donation will do and why I should donate.”

      “Understanding more about the website, what their contributions throughout the years have already helped them achieve. What their future goals are would motivate me more to donate or volunteer.”

      “The pages have the title, but other than that it is kind of confusing to find where you are. There aren’t any breadcrumbs to know where you are.”

Define & Ideate

We synthesized the data from our survey and interviews to help us start identifying how we can resolve the challenges facing our users goal's when it comes to volunteering and/or donating to UOL.

Affinity Diagram

We gathered and organized by our insights from our interviews and used an Affinity Diagram to organize our insights. These included their demographics and snippets of comments from our interviews. We scoped for common patterns between our users and conducted further dissection of notes and sorted them into groups.

Value Proposition

Utah’s Open Lands is updating their website to help potential donors and volunteers easily find the services they are looking for.

Empathy Map

After grouping our insights we wanted to gain a deeper understanding of how our user’s empathize with their time and money spent with UOL. We used an empathy map to distinguish our user’s various modes of perception and how they can connect with UOL.

From our deduced data, we discovered the needs for our site to address both pains and gains expressed from our user. However, we desired to stay centered on our specific user so we began with composing a user persona.


User Persona

Using our knowledge from our affinity map, value proposition and empathy map we created a user persona. Hello to Savannah Peterson!

Now with a better understanding with who our UOL user is, their pains and gains. We began sketching our wireframes.

Lo-fi Wireframe Sketches

Our wireframes focused on the main pages our users would interact with.

Task Flow

In order to understand the journey of our users, we needed to see the steps they would take while interacting with the site. It was straightforward yet differs on how they both will introduce and interact with the different tasks.

Card Sorting

We started planning the architecture of the site with sorting cards and organizing the navigation in a way that would be intuitive for the users.

Sitemap

We finalized our card sorting, after we brainstormed how our user would logically search for information and based on our interviews the top pains that were expressed and searched for which are “what they do” and “who they are”. We restructured the architecture to prioritize these seeked pages on the site and what would follow logically.

Prototyping & Testing

To test the functionality of our app, we conducted usablity testing early into our protoype to ensure we could refine our app and iterate our final prototype to be functional and intutive to our users.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes & Prototype

Starting our prototype began with creating our wireframes using Adobe XD. Once we had created our wireframes, we used Adobe XD to make our limited functional prototype.

Usability Testing

Our usability testing was done remote via Zoom using A/B testing on the navigation components. We wanted to test if the navigation architecture was initially understood to be logical for our users based on interview insights. We recruited five participants to test our design’s functionality and navigation by stating having them complete different tasks. We formulated tasks to navigate top pain points which are donating, who they are, what they do. We set up two different prototypes with different IA to see which would be the most successful with our users. The results concluded the following:

      test B was preferred over test A
      test A was more successful amongst the group
      Donation UI/IA impacted how likely user would give donation

We asked our interviews to provide any feedback that could be improved upon with the Think Aloud method during tasks. The following feedback we received was to:

      Move Give tab
      Create consistent secondary navigation
      Make clickable elements obvious
      Make design elements flow throughout the prototype
      Add compelling imagery
      Remove pop-up from donate page
      Make fonts a little bigger
      Make headers consistent

Branding

Mood Board

We began seeking inspiration via Google search images and Pinterest. We searched minimal and modern along with nature and neutral colors to complement the existing UI elements that were being kept for UOL. This helped us find the modern element we wanted to incorporate into the style and guide the design.

Logo Design

We had to maintain the logo as it is originally per the guidance of our stakeholders, who mentioned it in our stakeholder interview.

UI Style Guide

After gathering the elements for UOL to complement the site, we composed a guide for future reference and then began to apply the style guide to our wireframes.

Prototype
